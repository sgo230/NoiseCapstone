Rapid Response
to Noise Complaints

Authors:

Duke Sung Hoon Yang, Qinyu Goh, Sam Ovenshine, Siddhanth Shetty, Zoe Martiniak

2019 Capstone Project

Supported by:

Center for Urban Science & Progress, New York University
New York City Department of Environmental Protection


In recent years, the growing volume of noise complaints in New York City has increased response times and hindered enforcement of the city’s noise code by the Department of Environmental Protection. In this Capstone project, we used supervised learning to assign complaint priority in order to optimize response to noise complaints.

Problem Statement

The primary motivation of this project is to provide a technological approach to improve the current state of affairs. More specifically, the main goal is to showcase—visually, on a map in real time—areas where there are higher probabilities of verifying noise complaints so resources can be directed to such areas at the discretion of the inspector coordinator.
